Damn-e-koh

Daman-e-Koh is a popular hilltop garden and panoramic viewpoint situated in the center of the Margalla Hills National Park in Islamabad, Pakistan. Poetically translated from Persian as the "foothills" or "lap of the mountain," this scenic attraction sits at an elevation of approximately 2,400 feet above sea level and 500 feet directly above the capital city. It serves as a major recreational hub and a midpoint on the route up to the higher Pir Sohawa peak. The viewpoint offers visitors a breathtaking, bird's-eye panorama of Islamabad's modern grid layout, featuring clear views of iconic landmarks such as the Faisal Mosque, Rawal Lake, and 7th Avenue. Managed and modernized with paved pathways, terraced gardens, and observation decks equipped with telescopes, it provides a cool, tranquil escape from the urban hustle. The park is also famous for its local wildlife, particularly the resident Rhesus monkeys that frequently interact with tourists. Offering local food stalls, souvenir shops, and well-lit evening views, it stands as a must-visit destination for families, hikers, and photographers capturing the capital city.
